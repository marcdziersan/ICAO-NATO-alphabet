📖 About the ICAO / NATO / IMO Spelling Alphabet

The International Radiotelephony Spelling Alphabet, commonly known as the NATO phonetic alphabet, is the most widely used spelling alphabet worldwide. It was developed by the International Civil Aviation Organization (ICAO) and later adopted by the North Atlantic Treaty Organization (NATO) and the International Maritime Organization (IMO).

This standardized alphabet ensures clear and unambiguous communication in critical environments where radio transmissions may be distorted or misunderstood.

✈️

ICAO

International Civil Aviation Organization

Developed the alphabet in the 1950s for global aviation communication to ensure pilot-controller clarity.

🛡️

NATO

North Atlantic Treaty Organization

Adopted and standardized the alphabet for military operations across all allied forces worldwide.

🚢

IMO

International Maritime Organization

Uses the same alphabet for maritime communication, including ship-to-ship and ship-to-shore radio.
